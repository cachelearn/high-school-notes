1. Inequalities

Wavy Curve Method

The single most powerful tool for solving rational/polynomial inequalities.

  1. Move everything to one side: $f(x) \gtrless 0$
  2. Factorise completely. Find all roots (numerator zeros) and poles (denominator zeros).
  3. Mark on the number line. Odd-power factors → sign changes at that point. Even-power factors → sign bounces (does NOT change).
  4. Rightmost region is positive (for positive leading coefficient), then alternate left.
  5. Include roots for $\geq / \leq$. Always exclude poles (denominator can't be zero).
Example: Solve $\dfrac{(x-1)(x-3)^2}{x+2} > 0$

Critical points: $x=-2$ (pole, exclude), $x=1$ (odd → changes), $x=3$ (even → bounces).
Sign from right: $+$ on $(3,\infty)$, $+$ on $(1,3)$, $-$ on $(-2,1)$, $-$ on $(-\infty,-2)$.
Answer: $\boldsymbol{x \in (1,3) \cup (3,\infty)}$
JEE Angle: Wavy curve appears in domains of functions, range problems, and inequation questions. Never cross-multiply a variable — use wavy curve instead.

3. Logarithms

$\log_b x$ is defined only when $\boldsymbol{x > 0}$ and $\boldsymbol{b > 0,\, b \neq 1}$.

Laws of Logarithm

LawFormula
Product$\log_b(mn) = \log_b m + \log_b n$
Quotient$\log_b(m/n) = \log_b m - \log_b n$
Power$\log_b(m^k) = k\log_b m$
Base change$\log_b m = \dfrac{\log_a m}{\log_a b}$
Reciprocal base$\log_b a \cdot \log_a b = 1$
Chain$\log_b a \cdot \log_a c = \log_b c$
Power on base$\log_{b^k} m = \tfrac{1}{k}\log_b m$
Identity$a^{\log_a x} = x$
Exponential form$\log_b m = x \Leftrightarrow b^x = m$

Log Inequalities

$$\log_b f(x) > \log_b g(x) \Leftrightarrow \begin{cases} f(x) > g(x) & \text{if } b > 1 \\ f(x) < g(x) & \text{if } 0 < b < 1 \end{cases}$$

Always also impose: $f(x) > 0$ and $g(x) > 0$.

JEE Angle: Domain restriction is the #1 trap in log problems. Write domain conditions first, every time.
Example: Solve $\log_{0.5}(x^2-5) > \log_{0.5}(4)$
Base $<1$ → flips: $x^2-5 < 4$. Domain: $x^2 > 5$.
Answer: $\boldsymbol{x \in (-3,-\sqrt5) \cup (\sqrt5,3)}$

5. AM–GM & Classic Inequalities

$$\frac{a_1+\cdots+a_n}{n} \geq \sqrt[n]{a_1\cdots a_n} \geq \frac{n}{\frac{1}{a_1}+\cdots+\frac{1}{a_n}}$$

Equality holds when all terms are equal. Strategy: force the product to be constant, then AM $\geq$ GM gives the minimum.

Example: Minimize $2x + \dfrac{8}{x}$ for $x>0$.
$2x + \tfrac{8}{x} \geq 2\sqrt{16} = \mathbf{8}$. Equality at $x=2$.

Order Comparison (as $x \to 0^+$)

Higher-order infinitesimals vanish faster. The lowest-order term dominates in a sum.

Equivalent infinitesimalOrder
$\sin x \sim x$,   $\tan x \sim x$,   $e^x-1 \sim x$,   $\ln(1+x)\sim x$1
$1 - \cos x \sim \dfrac{x^2}{2}$2
$\tan x - \sin x \sim \dfrac{x^3}{2}$3

As $x\to\infty$:   $\ln x \ll x^k \ll e^x$ for any $k>0$.

JEE Angle: Use order comparison to instantly discard negligible terms in a limit, no algebra needed. If two infinitesimals have different orders, the lower-order one dominates.

L'Hôpital's Rule

For $\dfrac{0}{0}$ or $\dfrac{\infty}{\infty}$ forms:

$$\lim_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x\to a}\frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$$

For $1^\infty$ form: write $\lim f(x)^{g(x)} = e^{\lim g(x)\cdot\ln f(x)}$, then apply.

Vieta's Formulas

For $ax^2+bx+c=0$ with roots $\alpha,\beta$:

  • $\alpha+\beta = -b/a$,   $\alpha\beta = c/a$
  • $\alpha^2+\beta^2 = (\alpha+\beta)^2-2\alpha\beta$
  • $|\alpha-\beta| = \sqrt{(\alpha+\beta)^2-4\alpha\beta}$

Rationalization

Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate to clear surds. Essential for limits.

Example: $\lim_{x\to1}\dfrac{\sqrt{x}-1}{x-1}$ — multiply by $\dfrac{\sqrt{x}+1}{\sqrt{x}+1}$  →  $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}+1}\bigg|_{x=1} = \boldsymbol{\dfrac{1}{2}}$
