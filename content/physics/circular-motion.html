2. Centripetal Acceleration & Force

$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \omega^2 r, \qquad F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = m\omega^2 r$$

Centripetal force is not a new force. It is the net resultant of real forces (tension, gravity, normal, friction) pointing toward the centre. Never add it as an extra force in FBD.

3. Radial & Tangential Force Resolution

For any circular motion, always resolve forces into two directions:

DirectionEquationPoints toward
Radial (centripetal)$\sum F_r = \dfrac{mv^2}{r} = m\omega^2 r$Centre of circle
Tangential$\sum F_t = ma_t = mr\alpha$Along velocity direction
JEE Angle: A very common mistake is resolving forces only vertically and horizontally. Always set up your axes as radial (inward) and tangential (along motion) for circular motion problems. For example in vertical circle problems — at any angle $\theta$ from bottom, gravity has both a radial component ($mg\cos\phi$) and tangential component ($mg\sin\phi$) depending on the position.

4. Standard Circular Motion Systems

Conical Pendulum

$$T\cos\theta = mg, \quad T\sin\theta = \frac{mv^2}{r}, \quad r = L\sin\theta$$

$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{g}{L\cos\theta}}, \qquad T_{period} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L\cos\theta}{g}}$$

Vertical Circle

PositionTensionMin speed to complete loop
Bottom$T_B = mg + mv_B^2/r$$v_B \ge \sqrt{5gr}$
Top$T_T = mv_T^2/r - mg$$v_T \ge \sqrt{gr}$ (so $T_T \ge 0$)
Side$T_S = mv_S^2/r$—

Energy conservation: $\frac{1}{2}mv_B^2 = \frac{1}{2}mv_T^2 + mg(2r)$

Vehicle on Flat Curved Road

Friction provides centripetal force: $v_{max} = \sqrt{\mu rg}$

Vehicle on Banked Road

$$v_{ideal} = \sqrt{rg\tan\theta} \qquad \text{(no friction needed)}$$

With friction:

$$v_{max} = \sqrt{rg\cdot\frac{\tan\theta+\mu}{1-\mu\tan\theta}}, \qquad v_{min} = \sqrt{rg\cdot\frac{\tan\theta-\mu}{1+\mu\tan\theta}}$$

Example: Banked road $\theta=30°$, $r=100$ m, $g=10$.
$v_{ideal} = \sqrt{100\times10\times\tan30°} \approx \mathbf{24\ \text{m/s}}$

JEE Angle: The critical condition is $T=0$ at the top. The boundary $v_B = \sqrt{2gr}$ corresponds to the ball just reaching the horizontal level of the centre.

6. Centrifugal Force (Rotating Frame)

In the rotating frame, a pseudo force $mv^2/r$ acts outward. It is not real — exists only in the rotating frame.

7. Object on Rotating Platform / Table

A block at radius $r$ on a rotating platform (angular velocity $\omega$). Friction provides centripetal force:

$$f = m\omega^2 r \le \mu mg$$

Maximum radius before slipping:

$$r_{max} = \frac{\mu g}{\omega^2}$$

Maximum angular velocity for block to stay:

$$\omega_{max} = \sqrt{\frac{\mu g}{r}}$$

JEE Angle: "A coin is placed at radius $r$ on a rotating table — find max $\omega$ before it slides" is a very common JEE Main question.
Example: Coin at $r=0.5$ m, $\mu=0.4$, $g=10$. Find max $\omega$.
$\omega_{max} = \sqrt{0.4\times10/0.5} = \sqrt{8} \approx \mathbf{2.83\ \text{rad/s}}$

8. Motion in Vertical Cylinder (Death Well)

$$N = \frac{mv^2}{r}, \quad f = mg, \quad f \le \mu N \implies v_{min} = \sqrt{\frac{rg}{\mu}}$$
